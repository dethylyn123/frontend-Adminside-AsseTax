Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Frequently Asked Questions

Assetax is a cutting-edge digital solution for managing real property tax, designed to streamline the tax declaration process for both local governments and property owners.

Assetax leverages technology to automate tasks involved in real property tax management, such as data entry, calculation, and record-keeping. It provides a user-friendly interface for property owners to submit declarations and for tax assessors to review and assess taxes.

Assetax ensures transparency and accuracy by automating tedious tasks, eliminating errors associated with manual data entry, and providing a clear audit trail of all declarations and calculations.

  • The assessment sheet page is where property assessors input various details about a property, such as its location, size, structure, amenities, and other relevant factors.
  • This information is used to calculate the property's market value and determine its assessed value for taxation purposes.
  • The page often includes fields for inputting property data, which are then processed using predetermined formulas or algorithms to calculate the property's assessed value.
  • The assessed value derived from the assessment sheet helps determine the property owner's tax obligations.

  • The form declaration page is where property owners provide official declarations and information about their properties for tax assessment purposes.
  • Property owners typically fill out predefined forms with details such as property location, size, use, improvements, and other relevant data.
  • This information is submitted to the tax authorities for assessment and verification.
  • The tax authorities may review the submitted declarations and conduct assessments based on the provided information to determine the property's taxable value and applicable taxes.
